Geospatial Major Project
The Geospatial Major Project is a year-long project that occurs in the final year of the geospatial science/surveying degree. The project is split up into two components:
  1. Geospatial Major Project A: Creating the proposal and researching the proposed topic
  2. Geospatial Major Project B: Gathering data, results and writing up the report

The topic that my project will be covering is mapping the flesh-footed shearwater across the Recherche archipelago using remote sensing and species distribution modelling and I am supervised by both Mariela Soto-Berelov and Simon Jones.

The project involves using the distribution modelling program called MAXENT to determine the distribution of the birds based on points the birds have been sighted from literature and from data obtained from the Recherche archipelago by Jennifer Lavers (Univeristy of Tasmania). The software that I have used in the assignment has been ArcMap and QGIS to create feature classes of the points and coordinates the birds have been sighted and to crop and extract raster datasets of independent variables that can affect the distribution, being the temperature and rainfall of the archipelago obtained at WorldClim.
