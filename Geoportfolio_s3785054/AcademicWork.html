Academic Portfolio Overview
Throughout my four years at RMIT, I have undertaken a range of projects which have challenged my ability to apply the skills and knowledge I have learned in class. I have completed assignments that have utilised the geospatial skills of using Geographical Information System (GIS) programs to perform analysis of spatial data, Remote Sensing and Photogrammetry analysis of satellite imagery, using drones to capture and map a landscape and use cartographic principles to create legible maps based on a chosen topic.

The three most significant projects that I have undertaken as a geospatial science student along with the associated subject are:

  1. Mapping the flesh-footed shearwater across the Recherche archipelago using remote sensing and species distribution modelling (Geospatial Major Project)
  2. Creating a Digital Twin of Honiara's built environment (Advanced SIS)
  3. Amphitheatre Proposal at Deep Rock Oval, Yarra Bend Park (Applied Geospatial Techniques)
