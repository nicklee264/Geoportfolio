Unmanned Aerial System Operations
Unmanned Aerial System Operations involves both the theory and practical application of knowledge about flying drones to capture aerial imagery. While this subject is not a geospatial science or surveying subject, the knowledge can be strongly applied to Photogrammetry if one wishes to obtain a drone license to work on the field in the collection of aerial imagery.

The major assignment of the course involves a group project at the Greensborough Model Aircraft Club where a drone will fly based on the pre-programmed flight path created by the students. The drone will capture aerial imagery of the terrain where the assignment is to create the following deliverables:

  • Flight plans used for the drone
  • Property map of the area in the form of an orthomosaic
  • Elevation map of the area
  • 3D model of the creek with elevation provided
