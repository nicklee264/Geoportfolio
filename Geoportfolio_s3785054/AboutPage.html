About the author

As of the year 2022, Nicholas Lee is a final year undergraduate Geospatial Science (BH117) student. He started studying RMIT in 2019 as a first year undergraduate student. A big portion of Nicholas' life has revolved around maps starting from when he played Pokémon on his Game Boy Advance and reading a lot of the game guides, all of which involved maps of the individual Routes. When Nicholas took up orienteering as a summer sport in high school from years 2013 to 2018, that's where he learned (the hard way) the importance of being able to read a map to get from one place to another. When Nicholas started studying geography between years 9 to 12 in high school, he began to take an interest in learning more about mapping, specifically the technologies used to obtain map data and the process of how maps are made.

The interests Nicholas has in the course is learning about the technologies used behind obtaining spatial data and the creation of maps, hoping to find an occupation associated with satellite imagery, remote sensing and computer based mapping. Nicholas wishes to travel the world to make a map of the landmarks he has travelled..... Along with taking photos with his own mark to prove where he has been.
